## &nbsp;About

I build the parts of software that stay invisible when they work — APIs, data models, background processing, and the infrastructure that holds a product together under real load. I care less about which framework is trending and more about *why* systems are built the way they are: how they fail, how they recover, and how they keep earning trust as they scale.

Right now I'm deepening my engineering foundation through Microsoft's **Back-End Developer Professional Certificate**, moving into the **C#** and **.NET** ecosystem while I keep shipping backend systems in **TypeScript** and **Node.js**. Every project I take on is a step toward the same thing: writing software that solves an actual business problem, not a demo.

## &nbsp;Engineering Philosophy

<table width="100%">
<tr>
<td width="33%" valign="top">

**Reliability first**

Software earns trust by behaving predictably — especially when things go wrong. I design for failure before I design for features.

</td>
<td width="33%" valign="top">

**Depth over speed**

I'd rather understand a system completely than assemble one I can't reason about. Fundamentals compound; shortcuts don't.

</td>
<td width="33%" valign="top">

**Business, not just code**

Every system exists to solve a real problem. The best architecture is the one that keeps delivering value long after launch.

</td>
</tr>
</table>
